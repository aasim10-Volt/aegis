# Product

## Register

product

## Users

University faculty — capstone supervisors/lecturers and program admins (CIPHER 2.0). Their
context: running an allocation, triaging engagement risks, and auditing governance at a desk,
often demoing the result to colleagues or stakeholders. Students are downstream; the dashboard
is faculty/admin-facing.

## Product Purpose

AEGIS turns a cohort + project proposals into fair teams (evidence-weighted scoring, SPA
matching, maximin balance), then surfaces engagement risks (disengagement, uneven workload,
burnout) and a tamper-evident governance trail. Success: a supervisor runs an allocation and,
within seconds, trusts the result and sees exactly what needs attention — credible enough to put
in front of stakeholders.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, trustworthy, premium. Voice is human and professional — never developer jargon.
It should read as a governance/decision tool with Linear/Vercel-grade restraint, not a toy.
Three words: composed, credible, clear.

## Anti-references

Developer-looking admin panels (raw IDs like STU_05 / T::P_05, code slashes, monospace
everywhere). AI-slop dashboards: identical icon-heading-text card grids, gradient text,
decorative glassmorphism, the big-number hero-metric template. Harsh 1px-everywhere borders,
cramped spacing, neon. Bootstrap-default looks.

## Design Principles

- **Trust through clarity** — every score and number is legible and, where it matters, explained;
  no decoration that obscures meaning.
- **Human language** — surface project/person names and plain wording, never internal codes.
- **Calm hierarchy** — the one thing needing attention is obvious; everything else recedes.
- **Status is meaning, not decoration** — green/amber/red only ever encode health, never style.
- **Demo-ready restraint** — premium comes from spacing, type, and hierarchy, not effects.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: body text ≥ 4.5:1, large text ≥ 3:1, verified in **both** light and dark themes. Light
and dark are both first-class. Every animation has a reduced-motion fallback. Colour is never the
only signal — status always pairs with a label and dot/icon.
